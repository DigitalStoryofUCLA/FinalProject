Background

As seniors at UCLA, who got deprived of 18 months of our college experience, we are even more excited to be back on campus for our final year of college. Looking back at what this institution has given us socially, academically and professionally, the three of us were interested in taking a deeper dive into the founding and creation of UCLA. As our university just celebrated its 100 years, we want to take a step back and acknowledge those who have made UCLA what it is today and shaped our experiences. We are extremely thankful to be a part of the Bruin community and thus have chosen to tell our own digital story of the University of California, Los Angeles.

Introduction

UCLA was founded in 1919, in the heart of West Los Angeles, as one of the southern branches of the University of California, nowadays known as Berkeley. This school, that we proudly call our alma mater, is home to 32,000 undergraduate, 12,000 graduate and over 8,000 faculty members. The one hundred years of history span multiple generations and our institution has persevered during a world war, multiple economic recessions and a global pandemic. For our final project, we aim to tell the story of the University of California, Los Angeles in chronological order, from its inception in 1919 to current day. As we now form part of UCLA‚Äôs history, we aim to incorporate our own experiences into this narrative to pay tribute and recapitulate the past century worth of groundbreaking events and strides our school has been a part of.

Significance

We hope that this webpage can be used as a resource and tool for all Bruins. The importance of understanding our institution's history cannot be understated as it frames and informs present day events. Current students, faculty, and alumni should possess knowledge of the key events that led UCLA to the world-renouned university that it is today to be grounded in our academic pursuits.

Moreover, UCLA is becoming increasingly selective, as it was ranked the #1 public university in the United States for consecutive years by U.S. News & World Reports. This, along with our world-class professors and excellence among a variety of acacemic subjects, has UCLA on track to reach single-digit acceptance for Fall 2022 freshmen. We hope to broaden opportunity by informing prospective students on not only our institution's history, but also on the student experience and academic requirements.¬†
